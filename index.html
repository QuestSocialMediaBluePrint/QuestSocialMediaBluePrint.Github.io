Level Up Your VR Go-To-Market Strategy: A Quest App Marketing Blueprint

By Rohan Pandula, Developer Strategist @ Meta.

Over my tenure at Meta, serving in the Developer Relations team for non-gaming applications on the Quest platform, I have had the opportunity to learn from and collaborate with some of the most successful application developers in the industry. Drawing upon these interactions, as well as my extensive background in marketing, I have developed this framework as a comprehensive guide to effectively launch and sustain user engagement for your Quest application via social media.

Introduction

Social media provides invaluable opportunities for VR developers to build awareness, drive user acquisition, and sustain engagement for their apps. This blueprint outlines a phased approach across pre-launch, launch, and post-launch to leverage social platforms and influencers to successfully market a Quest application launch.

Phase 1: Pre-Launch Phase

In Phase 1, your goal is to create a solid foundation of understanding your audience and fostering a community around your Quest application's development process. It's also about forging relationships with creators who can help introduce your title to a larger audience.

Understand Your Audience:

Gaining visibility for your Quest application won't come automatically; you need to be proactive. Start by conducting market research to understand who your potential users are. This active impression generation involves considering demographic details such as age, location, interests, and preferences. Your go-to-market strategy should begin here. Use this information to guide your decisions on application design, aesthetics, mechanics, and how to best communicate with your audience.

  • Conduct Market Research: Analyze target demographics, interests, behaviors on key platforms.
  • Create Social Accounts: Establish branded accounts and share development updates.
  • Enable Wishlisting: On Meta Quest store to build intent audience. Remarket to them.
  • Foster Community: Create a Discord server for superfans. Host AMAs and gather feedback.
  • Identify Influencers: Reach out to influencers whose audience matches yours. Provide early access.
  • Develop Video Assets: Create trailers, teasers, clips tailored for platforms.
  • PR Outreach: Craft press releases and pitch to VR publications. Offer early access.
  • Collect Email List: Offer newsletter signup to share updates.
  • Start Paid Ads: Run limited ads to relevant target demographics.

Building in Public:

Embrace a 'Building in Public' approach, fostering transparency in your development process. Regularly share updates across social media or blogs, even before your application is fully developed or launched. This not only builds anticipation but strengthens user-developer connections. It opens a dialogue for valuable feedback, turning users into advocates and extending your reach.

Establish a Discord Server:

Establish a Discord server for your application, creating a hub for fans and developers. Utilize this popular platform to consistently share updates, gather feedback, host Q&As and events. This engagement fosters community and builds your 'impression muscle' for sustained success. Ensure civil, on-topic discussions.

Identify Potential Creators:

Identify creators, influencers or streamers whose audience aligns with yours to amplify reach. Authentically engage them to create content like videos, reviews or tutorials. Remember, their authenticity is invaluable. Rather than push for overly positive reviews, inspire honest impressions - these carry more weight.

Phase 2: Launch Phase

In Phase 2, further cultivate relationships with your community and creators. Engage your audience, incorporate feedback to improve your application. Teasers and promotions add launch hype. Beta testing ensures quality expectations are met.

  • Share Sneak Peeks: Post clips and behind-the-scenes looks to build existing audience hype.
  • Influencer Content: Collaborate on videos showcasing key features.
  • Community Contests: Organize giveaways on Discord and social platforms.
  • Expand Paid Ads: Widen reach, focus messaging on value proposition.
  • Offer Pre-Ordering: On Meta Quest store to convert hype into sales. Discount pre-orders.
  • Press Outreach: Pitch publications for launch reviews. Offer exclusive preview access.
  • Ramp Up Discord: Drive launch excitement on Discord.
  • Engage with Your Audience:

Engage with Your Audience:

Once you have a community, keep them engaged. Regularly share progress updates, sneak peeks into upcoming features, behind-the-scenes looks into your development process. Ask for and implement reasonable feedback suggestions to show you value opinions. Consider contests or events to further enhance community engagement. Maintaining a genuine, continuous dialogue is key.

Work with Creators:

Now initiate collaborations with the creators identified in Phase 1. Provide early access, suggest content ideas, invite them to exclusive developer interviews. Maintain a respectful, mutually beneficial relationship.

Pre-Launch Teasers and Promotions:

As launch approaches, release teasers - trailers, screenshots or clips - to build anticipation. Consider pre-order discounts, early access benefits or digital bonuses. Encourage purchase once released.

Beta Testing and Feedback:

Beta testing ensures application quality before launch. Inviting community members to test helps identify bugs and makes them feel connected to the process. Be open to feedback and make improvements. Their hands-on experience provides insights into mechanics and overall user experience.

Phase 3: Post-Launch Phase

In Phase 3, amplify engagement within your established community, continue launch momentum. Promote UGC, implement challenges and creator codes, emphasize social reinforcement. Sustain visibility in a competitive market. Maintain users' sense of ownership and contribution - this is instrumental to long term success.

  • Highlight UGC: Reshare videos, screenshots and discussions showcasing your application.
  • Foster Loyalty: Spotlight power users. Offer exclusive access, discounts or early previews.
  • Respond to Users: Publicly interact with feedback and reviews. Provide support.
  • Influencer Initiatives: Plan content updates and feature drops.
  • Community Contests: Continuously organize creative contests.
  • Paid Ads: Retarget engaged users to promote expansions and new content.
  • Promote User-Generated Content:

Promote User-Generated Content:

Share and promote UGC - videos, fan art, reviews. This validates community effort and provides authentic promotion. It shows genuine enthusiasm and engagement, encouraging more UGC.

Run Challenges or Contests:

Introduce in-application challenges or contests to stimulate engagement and re-engagement. Make them achievable solo or cooperative. Offer rewards like exclusive items or recognition. This fosters a competitive spirit, encouraging repeat use and mindfulness.

Implement Creator Codes:

Creator codes incentivize influencers to promote your application. Offer exclusive in-app items or discounts, enticing their followers. Reach out to influencers whose audience aligns with yours.

Encourage Social Reinforcement:

Implement features for sharing in-app moments and cooperative play rewards. This facilitates a social, community experience boosting retention and engagement. Users are more likely to stay if they can share experiences. Make this easy to maintain the "impression muscle".

In conclusion, marketing a Quest application on social media needs a three-phase approach tying together audience understanding, transparent development and genuine user/creator relationships. This journey transforms users into engaged advocates, ensuring visibility and long-term success in a competitive landscape.
